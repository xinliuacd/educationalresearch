About

Hello! I am Dr Xin Liu, Associate Professor and Master’s Supervisor at the College of Teacher Education, Ningbo University. My research sits at the intersection of educational psychology, mathematics education, teacher education, and international comparative education, with a strong focus on using large-scale assessment and survey data (e.g., PISA, TALIS, TIMSS).

Research interests
  • Teaching quality and instructional practices
  • Student motivation, emotions (e.g., anxiety), and well-being
  • Educational equity and digital inclusion
  • Teacher professional development and evaluation
  • Cross-national and cross-cultural comparison
Methods
  • Structural equation modelling (SEM) & latent variable models
  • Multilevel modelling & cross-level interactions
  • Measurement invariance and scale validation
  • Mediation/moderation and conditional process models
  • Trend and longitudinal analyses (including PV/weight handling)

Projects & Grants

Below is an integrated list (English CV + updated items after 2024). Major funded projects are described in more detail.

The multi-level mechanisms of digital device use on teaching quality and educational equity from a global perspective PI

2025–2028 National Social Science Fund of China (Education) — General Project Project No. BHA250113
  • Builds a “digital devices → teaching quality → learning outcomes → equity” mechanism framework using global large-scale data.
  • Emphasises multilevel modelling, cross-country comparison, and policy-relevant interpretations for digital education.

From Tiger Moms to Free-Range Parenting: Anxiety and Achievement among Chinese and Nordic Learners PI

2025–2027 Ningbo University Special Talent Project Project No. ZX2025000345
  • Investigates how learning environments shape anxiety and achievement across Chinese and Nordic learner groups.
  • Combines large-scale datasets (e.g., PISA) with advanced modelling (multilevel SEM, mediation/moderation) to inform practice.

Co-constructing mathematics motivation in primary education – A longitudinal study in six European countries Norway team PI

2021–2024 Research Council of Norway (FINNUT) Grant No. 301033
  • Coordinated Norwegian team work, supported data collection/management, and led cross-country analyses and dissemination.
