About

Hello! I am Dr. Xin Liu. I am an Associate Professor at the College of Teacher Education, Ningbo University. My research sits at the intersection of educational psychology, mathematics education, teacher education, and international comparative education.

Research focus

  • Teaching quality and instructional practices
  • Student motivation, emotions (especially mathematics anxiety), and well-being
  • Educational equity and digital inclusion
  • Cross-national and cross-cultural comparisons

Methods

  • Structural equation modelling (SEM) and latent variable models
  • Multilevel modelling and cross-level interactions
  • Measurement invariance and scale validation
  • Mediation / moderation (conditional process) models

The multi-level mechanisms of digital device use on teaching quality and educational equity from a global perspective (2025–2028)
National Social Science Fund of China (Education) — General Project • Project No. BHA250113 • PI

Building a multi-level mechanism framework to explain how digital device use relates to teaching quality, learning outcomes, and equity across education systems.

From Tiger Moms to Free-Range Parenting: Anxiety and Achievement among Chinese and Nordic Learners (2025–2027)
Ningbo University Special Talent Project • Project No. ZX2025000345 • PI

Comparative research on anxiety and achievement across Chinese and Nordic contexts, focusing on learning environments and family–school support mechanisms.

Co-constructing mathematics motivation in primary education: A longitudinal study in six European countries (2021–2024)
Research Council of Norway (FINNUT) • Grant No. 301033 • Norway team PI / coordinator

International collaboration investigating motivational processes in primary mathematics education, integrating survey and classroom data.
