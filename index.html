ABOUT ME

Improve the quality and equity of education through data-driven empirical research.

Hi! I am Xin Liu, an Associate Professor at the College of Teacher Education, Ningbo University (China). My academic journey spans Asia and Europe: I received my PhD in Educational Sciences from Ghent University (Belgium) and Master of Science in Educational Research from the University of Gothenburg (Sweden). I completed a Postdoctoral Research Fellowship in Educational Psychology at the University of Oslo (Norway). Before moving abroad, I worked as a Lecturer at Daqing Vocational College and earned three bachelor's degrees in China : a Bachelor of Arts, a Bachelor of Education, and a Bachelor of Engineering.

My research specialises in secondary analyses of international large-scale educational assessments (e.g., PISA, TALIS, TIMSS) to understand how student, teacher, and school factors shape learning outcomes (both achievement and affective outcomes), with a particular focus on teaching quality, mathematics education, teacher education, ICT role, and educational equity across cultural contexts.

Methodologically, I work with quantitative approaches including latent variable modelling, structural equation modelling, multilevel and longitudinal analyses, mediation/moderation, and mixture modelling.
