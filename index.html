About

Hello! I am Dr. Xin Liu (刘鑫), an Associate Professor and Master's Supervisor at the College of Teacher Education, Ningbo University (China).

My research focuses on educational psychology, mathematics education, teacher education, and international comparative research using large-scale datasets (e.g., PISA, TALIS, TIMSS). Methodologically, I work with structural equation modelling, multilevel modelling, measurement invariance, and trend/longitudinal analyses.

Research interests

  • Teaching quality and instructional practices
  • Student motivation, emotions (e.g., anxiety), and well-being
  • Educational equity and digital inclusion
  • Teacher professional development and evaluation

Methods

  • SEM / latent variable models
  • Multilevel models & cross-level interactions
  • Measurement invariance
  • Trend and longitudinal analyses

CURRENT PROJECTS

Global digital device use, teaching quality and educational equity (2025–2028)
National Social Science Fund of China (Education) — Grant No. BHA250113 (PI).

Using PISA 2022 global data, the project models how digital device access and use shape teaching quality and educational equity, and translates cross-national evidence into policy implications for China.

Co-constructing mathematics motivation in primary education: a longitudinal study in six European countries (2021–2024)
Research Council of Norway (FINNUT) — Grant No. 301033 (Co-PI; PI for the Norwegian team).

A six-country mixed-methods project examining pupils' mathematics motivation and emotions, teachers' practices and beliefs, and parents' involvement in mathematics learning.

From Tiger Moms to Free-Range Parenting: anxiety and achievement among Chinese and Nordic learners (2025–2027)
Ningbo University — Project No. ZX2025000345 (PI).

A cross-cultural project linking family processes with students' academic emotions (especially anxiety) and achievement across Chinese and Nordic contexts.
